Maya was a girl who always had something critical to say. She said Nan's hair was messy, Ton's shoes were old, and Aow's bag was a dull color. Her friends heard it and went quiet, but no one ever told Maya how much it hurt.

One day, her mother took her to an antique shop at the market. Among all the curious objects, Maya noticed a small mirror with a golden frame sitting in a corner.

"This mirror is special," the shopkeeper said softly. "It shows you what others see when they look at you."

Maya laughed and thought the woman was joking - but she bought it anyway out of curiosity.

Back home, she held it up and looked. At first she only saw her own face. But then the image slowly changed. She saw herself pointing at a friend with a scowl. She saw her friends standing far away, nobody wanting to sit beside her.

"That's not me!" Maya argued.

But the mirror kept showing more. She saw the moment she said Aow's drawing was bad - and the tears in Aow's eyes that she had tried to hide. She saw the moment she said Nan wasn't good at music - and how Nan had put down her violin and not picked it up again for days.

Maya stood still for a long time. Her heart grew heavier.

The next morning, she brought a box of colored pencils to Aow. "Want to draw together? I'd like to learn from you."

Aow looked surprised - but smiled and handed her a pencil.

That evening, Maya sat and listened to Nan play the violin. "That sounds really beautiful," she said, and she meant it.

Nan's cheeks went pink with happiness, and she played on with more confidence than before.

That night, Maya looked into the mirror one more time. The image had changed. Now she saw herself sitting beside her friends, everyone smiling, and the light around her was brighter than before.

The mirror wasn't magic because it changed others. It was magic because it helped Maya see that she herself was the one who needed to change.

**Moral:** Our words can build or break someone else's confidence. Before you criticize, ask yourself: if someone said this to me, how would I feel?
