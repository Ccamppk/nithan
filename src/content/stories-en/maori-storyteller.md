Once upon a time, on the lush green island of New Zealand, a Maori boy named Tane grew up hearing the stories of his ancestors that his grandfather told every night.

Maori stories weren't just tales — they were the history of the tribe, passed down by word of mouth through many generations.

On the day of the tribe's great gathering, Grandfather told Tane it was time for him to stand and tell a story in front of everyone.

Tane stood up at the gathering, looking out at hundreds of people sitting and waiting to listen. His heart dropped.

Every story he had memorized vanished completely.

He took a deep breath, and then said whatever rose up in his heart. "Once upon a time, there was a young man afraid to stand before people. He stood right here, just like I am now."

The crowd chuckled softly.

Tane kept going. He talked about his grandfather, who had taught him. He talked about the sun rising over the sea. He talked about the birds that sang every morning.

It wasn't a memorized story — it was the story he actually lived.

When he finished, Grandfather walked over and placed a hand on his shoulder. "You just told the best story I have ever heard, because it came from your heart."

Tane understood that a true story doesn't come from memory — it comes from the life we actually live.

**Moral:** The best stories are the ones that come from real experience and a sincere heart. You don't need to memorize or pretend — tell what you truly know and truly feel, and that is what lets others hear you and feel it too.
