High up in the sky, above the soft white clouds, there was a little house made of silver light. A small star named "Ting" lived there with her mother.

Every night, when the sun slipped below the horizon, the other stars would come out and shine joyfully. But Ting would burrow under her cloud blanket and refuse to go outside.

"Ting, come out and shine," Mama Star called.

"I don't want to," Ting replied in a small voice. "It's too dark out there."

Mama Star walked over and sat beside her daughter. "But it is a star's job to shine in the darkness, my love."

"What if I'm too scared?"

Mama smiled. "Then I'll tell you a story."

Ting pulled her fluffy white cloud blanket up to her chin. Mama settled in beside her, her voice soft and slow, like the gentlest of breezes.

"Once upon a time, long before you were born, the sky was very dark during the world's very first nights. Not a single star would come out — because every one of them was afraid of the dark, just as you are."

"Then who came out first?"

"The first star to come out was called 'Diaw.' He was tiny — smaller than the tip of a finger. But he thought to himself: if no one is brave enough, then someone has to be the first."

Ting listened carefully. Her eyes grew heavier.

"Diaw came out and shone his light — just a tiny glimmer. But the moment he did, the other stars began to find their courage too. One by one, then two, then ten, then a hundred, then a thousand, until the whole sky was filled with light."

"A tiny light could bring all those other lights?" Ting asked, her voice sleepy.

"Yes," said Mama, nodding. Her voice grew softer still. "And do you know — every night that you're tucked in under your cloud blanket, there are children down on the earth falling asleep too. They are not afraid of the dark, because they know a little star is watching over them."

Ting closed her eyes gently. "But I'm still inside..."

"That's all right," Mama said, wrapping her arms around her little star. "Tonight I'll shine in your place. Tomorrow we'll go out together. For now — just sleep."

A cool breeze drifted through the silver window. Ting breathed in slowly and deeply. The cloud blanket was warm and soft, like being held by the whole world.

"Mama..." Ting murmured.

"Yes, my love?"

"I love you."

Mama Star smiled the most gentle smile. "I love you more than every star in the sky."

Ting closed her eyes, slowly, slowly, and drifted off to sleep. Outside, Mama Star went to shine her light, bright and steady — keeping watch over her little one through the whole long night.

And that is why, every night, when children look up at the sky, they feel warm and find it easy to fall asleep. Because little Ting grows braver with each passing night, and even the smallest light she shines is enough to fill every child's heart with warmth.

**Moral:** A parent's love is a light that watches over us even while we sleep. No matter how dark the night may be, love is always there keeping guard. Sweet dreams tonight.
