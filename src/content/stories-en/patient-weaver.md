Once upon a time, in a village by the mountains, there lived a young woman named Nuan who dreamed of becoming the finest weaver in the land.

The day her elderly grandmother gave her an old loom before passing away, Nuan was overjoyed. But Grandmother told her, "This loom can only weave the truth. If your heart isn't pure, the cloth it produces will show you things you don't want to know."

Nuan didn't understand, but she accepted it with respect.

On the first day she sat down to weave, Nuan intended to create a beautiful flower pattern. But when the cloth unrolled, it showed a scene of her getting angry at her little sister and saying hurtful words.

Nuan wove again, this time intending red flowers. Instead, it showed her lying to her mother about staying out late to play.

Every piece of cloth showed something she didn't want to see.

Nuan grew angry, almost ready to burn the loom, but then she stopped and thought.

She sat down and began looking carefully at each image in the cloth.

The scene of her anger at her sister. The scene of lying to her mother. The scene of taking a friend's things without asking. The scene of doing good deeds just to be seen, not because she truly meant it.

Nuan sat looking at all the cloth for a long time, and then she cried.

Not out of anger at the loom, but because for the first time, she saw herself clearly.

She began to fix things, one by one. She apologized to her sister. She apologized to her mother. She returned her friend's belongings. And she began doing good because she truly wanted to, not to be seen by others.

A week later, she sat down to weave again.

This time, the cloth that emerged showed golden flowers blooming beautifully, exactly as she had intended from the very start.

Nuan looked at the cloth with tears in her eyes, knowing the loom hadn't changed — she was the one who had changed.

Nuan's cloth became known throughout the land, not because it was the most beautiful, but because everyone who received her weaving could feel the sincerity woven into every single thread.

**Moral:** A truly magical gift doesn't make us more beautiful or more skilled — it helps us see ourselves as we truly are. The person brave enough to face the truth within themselves and try to change is the most beautiful person of all.
