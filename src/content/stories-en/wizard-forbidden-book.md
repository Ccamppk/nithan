Once upon a time, in a school of magic atop a high stone mountain, there was a great library filled with thousands of books.

But one book, every teacher said, must never be touched. Its cover was black, locked shut with a golden chain, sitting on the highest shelf of the library, with a sign that read: "Absolutely Do Not Open."

A boy named Jit was more curious than anyone else in his class.

"Why is it forbidden?" he asked his teacher.

"Because the time isn't right yet," the teacher answered simply.

Jit asked again several times, and got the same answer every time.

One night, while everyone else was asleep, Jit snuck into the library, climbed up the bookshelf, and pulled down the black book.

The golden chain fell away on its own the moment his hand touched it.

Jit opened the book.

Inside, there were no magic letters, no secret formulas, no treasure maps.

There was only a small mirror embedded in the very first page.

Jit looked into the mirror.

He saw himself — but not himself as he was now. It was himself in the future, a young man standing in the middle of a village engulfed in flames, this very book in his hands, casting a spell from it. But the fire didn't go out — it spread further and further.

Then the mirror went dark.

Jit closed the book, his hands trembling.

The next morning, he went to find his teacher and confessed that he had opened the book.

The teacher didn't punish him, only asked, "What did you see?"

"I saw myself making a terrible mistake," Jit answered.

"That is exactly why you must wait until you're ready," the teacher said. "Some knowledge is too powerful for someone not yet ready to receive it. The golden chain wasn't there to keep others out — it was there to protect you from using it before your time."

Jit sat quietly for a long while, then nodded.

From that day on, he studied with great dedication and patience, never rushing, never skipping a single step.

And when he grew into an adult, the book opened for him once again, this time with no chain to break — because by then, he was truly ready.

**Moral:** Knowledge and power gained before we are ready can become dangerous. Waiting for the right time isn't weakness — it's wisdom, because the best things always require time and true readiness.
