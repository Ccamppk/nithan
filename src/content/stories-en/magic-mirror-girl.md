Long ago, in a small town at the foot of a mountain, there lived a girl named Manee who hated looking in the mirror.

Every time she looked at herself, she felt her nose was too big, her hair too curly, her teeth too gapped. Her friends always seemed prettier than her.

One day, while walking through the market, she came across an old vendor with a small mirror displayed on her stall. It had a wooden frame beautifully carved with a flower pattern.

"What's special about this mirror?" Manee asked.

"Why don't you look and find out yourself," the vendor smiled.

Manee picked up the mirror and looked at her reflection — but the image she saw was different.

She saw her own hands, carrying her mother's heavy grocery bags every day. She saw her mouth, smiling at her little brother every morning. She saw her legs, running to help a friend just last week. She saw her eyes, sparkling every time she read a book.

The images were so beautiful that Manee stood stunned for a moment.

"What am I seeing?" she asked the vendor.

"This mirror doesn't show your appearance," the vendor said softly. "It shows who you really are."

Manee looked into the mirror again, this time gazing longer, and slowly she noticed something she had never seen before — that a person's beauty doesn't lie in their appearance, but in what they do for others.

She bought the mirror and placed it beside her bed.

From that day on, whenever she felt unsure of herself, she would look into the mirror and remind herself that the most beautiful thing about her couldn't be seen with the naked eye.

**Moral:** True beauty isn't about outward appearance — it's about goodness of heart. Kindness, courage, and the love we give to others are what truly make a person beautiful.
