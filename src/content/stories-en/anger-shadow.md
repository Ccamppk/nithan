Lek's shadow was different from everyone else's.

When she was happy, her shadow skipped and danced. When she was sad, it curled up small. But when she was angry — her shadow turned dark and grew large, spreading across the walls and floor until it swallowed the light around her.

Lek knew her shadow changed with her feelings. But she didn't think it was a problem.

Until the day she lost her temper at school.

---

It started with a small thing. Her best friend Wan had accidentally bumped into her art project and smudged the painting she'd worked on for three days.

Lek's shadow turned black instantly.

"You ruined everything!" she snapped. "You're so clumsy! I don't want to be your friend anymore!"

Wan's face fell. She didn't say anything. She just picked up her bag and walked to the other side of the classroom.

Lek's shadow stretched large across the floor, dark and heavy.

---

At home that evening, Lek told her grandmother what happened.

Grandmother listened quietly, then pointed to the shadow on the wall.

"Look," she said.

Lek's shadow had faded to gray. Small and hunched.

"Your shadow shows what's inside," Grandmother said. "When you're angry, it gets big and dark. But right now it looks small."

"Because I feel bad," Lek said.

"Words that come from anger leave marks," Grandmother said. "Not on walls. On hearts. And hearts take longer to heal than paintings."

Lek was quiet.

---

The next morning, she found Wan before school started.

"I'm sorry," Lek said. "I was angry and I said things that weren't fair. You didn't mean to bump my painting."

Wan looked at her for a long moment.

"I'm sorry too," Wan said. "I should have been more careful."

They stood quietly for a moment.

"Is your painting okay?" Wan asked.

"I fixed it. It actually looks better now." Lek smiled. "I added clouds where the smudge was."

Wan smiled back, a little shyly.

Lek glanced down at her shadow. It had brightened, stretching long and light in the morning sun.

She didn't know if anger would ever stop making her shadow grow dark. But she knew now that the words that followed — those were up to her.

**Lesson:** Anger is a feeling we all have, and feelings aren't wrong. But the words and actions we choose when we're angry can leave marks that take a long time to heal. Taking a breath before we speak gives us the chance to choose more carefully.
