Fierce waves hammered the small fishing boat. The sky turned black and swallowed the sun in an instant. Mo, ten years old, and her younger brother "Tam," eight years old, gripped the railing so tightly their knuckles turned white.

"I'm going for help - don't leave the boat!" their father called, his voice cracking before he disappeared into the darkness.

But a spinning waterspout sucked the boat away faster than anyone could stop it. When the storm finally calmed, Mo and Tam found themselves drifting in front of an island - an island that appeared on none of the charts aboard the boat.

"What island is this?" Tam whispered.

The trees on shore were towering and dense, their leaves wider than beach umbrellas. But the strangest thing was that everything on the island seemed perfectly still. No birdsong. No wind. Not even the trees moved.

Mo decided to bring her brother ashore to search for fresh water. They walked into the forest and found an old stone house, its door standing wide open. Inside were a table, chairs, and a cooking pot - but no people. It was as if whoever lived here had stepped out just moments ago.

"Someone lives here, for sure," said Mo.

On the table lay an old rolled-up map. Mo unrolled it and found it was a map of this very island, with a marked path leading somewhere. In the center of the map was a drawing of a large stone, with words written beneath it: "Place both hands together - the light will show you the way home."

"It's like a puzzle game!" Tam said excitedly.

Mo and Tam followed the map - across a long vine bridge over a stream, into a cave sparkling with glowing crystals, and up a rocky cliff whose summit held a great boulder shaped like a heart.

Tam asked, "If we have to place two hands at the same time - does that mean both of us have to do it together?"

Mo nodded. They stood on either side of the boulder and pressed their palms down at the same moment.

The stone glowed gold. Then the whole island came alive. The trees swayed, the wind returned, birds began to sing. And most miraculous of all - a golden current of water rose from the sea, swirling into a glowing trail that pointed north.

"That's the way home!" Mo cried.

They ran back to the boat and let the golden current carry them swiftly across the sea. Before long, the lights of their home harbor appeared on the horizon. Their father stood at the dock, his face streaked with tears.

As he pulled his children into a fierce hug, he asked, "Where have you been?"

Mo and Tam looked at each other and smiled.

"On an adventure, Dad."

That night they told him everything. Their father listened with wide eyes, then nodded slowly. "The Sleeping Island... the old people in the village used to say it was real. No one believed them."

"We believe them now," said Tam, with the widest smile of his life.

**Moral:** Courage and working together are the most important things in times of difficulty. No matter what obstacle stands in your way, if you refuse to give up and face it side by side, you will always find a way through.
